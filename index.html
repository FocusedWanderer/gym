🦵 Knee Rehab — Patellar Tendinopathy

Do these daily, ideally before your workout as part of warmup. These are evidence-based protocols for patellar tendon pain.

Phase 1: Isometric Loading (Weeks 1-2)

Pain relief + tendon loading without aggravation. Hold each for 45 seconds.

Wall Sit (Isometric Hold)
Bodyweight 5 × 45 sec hold — back against wall, knees at ~60° (not deep)
⚡ Key: Keep knees above ankles, NOT past toes. Should feel work, not sharp pain. If painful, reduce knee bend angle.
Spanish Squat (Banded Isometric)
Band 4 × 45 sec hold — loop band behind knees, anchored to rack/post, lean back
⚡ This shifts load to the patellar tendon specifically. Gold standard rehab exercise. Use a sturdy band.
Terminal Knee Extensions (Banded)
Band 3 × 15 reps each leg — band behind knee, straighten from ~30° to full extension
⚡ Builds VMO strength (inner quad) which supports patellar tracking.

Phase 2: Slow Eccentric Loading (Weeks 3-6)

Progress here once isometrics are pain-free. Slow eccentrics rebuild tendon structure.

Slow Eccentric Step-Downs
Bodyweight 3 × 10 each leg — stand on step, slowly lower opposite foot (4 sec down)
⚡ Use a 4-6" step. The SLOW lowering is what matters. Step back up normally.
Eccentric Single-Leg Decline Squat
Bodyweight 3 × 10 each leg — heels elevated on plate/book, slow 4-sec descent
⚡ The decline angle specifically loads the patellar tendon. THE gold standard for patellar tendinopathy rehab.

💪 Elbow Rehab — Lateral Epicondylitis (Tennis Elbow)

Left elbow. Caused by high shooting volume (~20k rounds/2 months). Do daily.

Daily Protocol

Tyler Twist (FlexBar or Towel)
Band/Towel 3 × 15 reps — wring a rolled towel like wringing water out, slow eccentric on left wrist
⚡ #1 evidence-based exercise for lateral epicondylitis. If you can get a TheraBand FlexBar (green), even better. The eccentric wrist extension is the key movement.
Eccentric Wrist Extensions
Light DB (3-5 lb) 3 × 15 — forearm on knee, palm down, slowly lower weight over 4 sec
⚡ Use your lightest dumbbell. Lift with the other hand to the top, then slowly lower with the affected arm only.
Wrist Flexor Stretch
Bodyweight 3 × 30 sec — arm straight, palm up, gently pull fingers back with other hand
Wrist Extensor Stretch
Bodyweight 3 × 30 sec — arm straight, palm down, gently press hand down with other hand

⚠️ Shooting & Elbow Pain

Your volume jumped from ~1-2k to ~10k rounds/month. The recoil absorption + sustained grip is textbook overuse of wrist extensors. Consider: (1) reducing volume to ~5k/month while rehabbing, (2) using a compression strap just below the elbow during shooting, (3) icing after sessions for 15 min. If no improvement in 4-6 weeks of consistent rehab, a PT eval is strongly recommended.

📋 When to See a Professional

If either condition doesn't improve after 4-6 weeks of consistent rehab work, or if you get sharp/sudden pain, swelling, or locking — get a sports medicine eval. A good sports med doc or orthopedic PT can do imaging and hands-on assessment that I can't.
